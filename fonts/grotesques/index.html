gro­tesques

↑ top ↩ fonts

These are the quintessential sans-serifs. Grotesques are the very first kind to have appeared, since the late 19th century. One early example is Akzidenz-Grotesk from 1898, basically the mother of all sans serifs and i'd take it over Helvetica any day.
In the 1950's, the neo-grotesques appeared, with more neutral and refined (some could say. boring) shapes. The biggest example is Helvetica from 1957. Horizontal terminals, tall x-height, all that stuff.

math and physics

→ Gravity Points

A cool interactive simulation of orbiting points by Akimitsu Hamamuro.

→ Byrne's Euclid

Byrne's colorful and playful rendition of Euclid's Elements in an interactive website by Nicholas Rougeux.

→ Paper Models of Polyhedra

A site by Gijs Korthals Altes with a ton of printable guides for making polyhedrons out of paper!

→ Inspiral Web

An online spirograph by Nathan Friend!

→ 100,000 Stars

A wonderful tour of the 100,000 closest stars, coded by space enthusiasts at Google.

→ PhET Physics Simulations

A bunch of neat physics simulations by a team from the University of Colorado.

→ Theories of Everything, Mapped

A map of many explanations on how everything works, by Natalie Wolchover.

→ Visual Polyhedra

A site by David I. McCooey with many many many interactive 3d polyhedra

→ If the Moon Were Only 1 Pixel

An accurate representation of the Solar System by Josh Worth. Shows how empty space really is.

→ Pythagorean Theorem and its many proofs

A collection of 122 proofs of the Pythagorean Theorem by Alexander Bogomolny.

→ The Fifty-Nine Icosahedra

A guide by H. Coxeter on beautiful stellations of the icosahedron and how to find them.

→ Harmonices Mundi

Johannes Kepler's book on the orbits of the planets and their relation to geometry and harmony.

→ Online Fractal Creator

An online tree fractal creator by Michael McDaid.

→ Uniform tilings in hyperbolic plane

Lots of pretty pictures to be looked at.

→ The On-Line Encyclopedia of Integer Sequences

A database of over 300000 integer sequences and lots of info about them. Founded by N. Sloane in 1964.

favorite musicians

These are some musicians who i listen/listened to a lot. I recommend checking them out! (blue links lead to bandcamp and green links lead to spotify)

  • Radiohead: They've been making music since the 90s, mostly rock at first but their stuff is super varied with each album. I recommend OK Computer and Kid A, as well as the Radiohead Public Library for everything they've made since their start. Almost.
  • Ginger Root/Cameron Lew: Again, his music has changed from his early years, from "agressive elevator soul" to city pop. Some great albums are Rikki and City Slicker.
  • Polo & Pan: This duo makes absolute bops of french house; though i haven't listened to them in a while, you absolutely should right now. Caravelle and Gengis make great listens. Sadly, their stuff isn't in bandcamp.
  • Tame Impala: A psychedelic/indie/rock/pop/synth "band" (it's all just one guy called Kevin Parker, he's AWESOME!!!) His best stuff is Currents and The Slow Rush. But his stuff isn't in bandcamp either whyyyy
  • Tally Hall: Though they're on hiatus since 2011 and only made 2 albums (Marvin's Marvelous Mechanical Museum and Good & Evil) , they're both absolutely worth listening to, as well as their side project Miracle Musical/ミラクルミュージカル!
  • Zelma Stone: The thing that was originally here was "delightful bedroom pop". I can't really find a better descriptor. A good place to start is Dreamland.
  • Ahoy/Stuart Brown: He makes videogame documentaries on Youtube, as well as all the background music for them! I recommend listening to the bgms to The First Video Game and Big Boxes.
  • Posy: Like Ahoy, he also makes great Youtube videos about everything and nothing and the music that goes alongside them. Good tracks on Way Back II and Segmented Segments.
  • Tom Jobim: A classic bossa nova composer from the 50-60s, and his stuff is listened to the world over. Stoneflower and Wave are must-listens.
  • Alan Hawkshaw: They compose background music for TV and commercials and things (aka library music). Don't know exactly how I came across him but his music is surprisingly good! Luke Vibert's Nuggets 3 includes a bunch of his songs alonside more bgm goodness.
  • Jack Stauber: His music is... weirdly good and simple, i'd say. Can't really put it well into words. HiLo and Pop Food are great places to start!
  • Honorable Mentions: Astrud Gilberto, Duster, HOME, Macroblank, Chico Buarque, The Bob Davis Group, Gilberto Gil, João Gilberto, Dorothy Ashby, Walter Murphy, Sevish, Bill Wurtz and João Donato
